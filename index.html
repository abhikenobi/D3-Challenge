Correlations Discovered Between Average Income and Poverty rates in the United States

Usuing census data, a graph was able to be created (shown above) showing the relations betweent the different states' average incomes and their poverty rates.

Through just visual observation, it can be noted that there seems to be a strong negative correlation between income and poverty rates. Meaning that as a state's averge income increases, their poverty rates seem to decrease. This could be possibly due to the fact that an increase in a state's averge income would mean that the state's population as a whole have higher income and outliers like the "1%" are unlikely to affect this statistic. This, however, would require further study in order to make this claim definitively as well as study of more datapoints such as a state's average cost of living or minimum wage.
